Memorial Library Is Proud To Announce The Opening Of Its New Branch In Manchester
Monday, February 15, 2021

Memorial library is delighted to announce the opening of its new Manchester branch on Monday, February 15, 2021. The Manchester branch library will be part of the new Birch Community Centre located next to the Birchfields park at Brighton Grove Street. A great deal of planning has gone into the new branch’s innovative design to focus on service innovation in order to provide the highest levels of customer satisfaction.


Through the application of retail principles, the ‘Discovery Layout’ of the new shelving will help patrons more easily find the items they are looking for. “we are all very excited by the new layout and shelving design being incorporated into the new Manchester branch. Schoolhouse Products Inc. have helped to realise our vision for a new approach to public library service,” shares Mark Williams, Chief Librarian. “To say staff are excited about the opening of the new branch wolid be an understatement and we look forward to welcoming the first patrons to the Manchester library branch as next week.”

At the new branch, patrons will be able to access a broader range of self-service options including laptop lending, self-directed printing and access to library materials even when the library is closed. In addition, Memorial library is excited to be working with partners from community services as they develop joint programming which will include access to a reading garden.
